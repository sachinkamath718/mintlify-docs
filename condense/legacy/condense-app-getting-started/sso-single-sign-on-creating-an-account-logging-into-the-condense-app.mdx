The Single Sign-On (SSO) feature in Condense allows users to authenticate seamlessly using their existing corporate or third-party identity provider credentials, eliminating the need for multiple login credentials. Currently, Condense supports SSO via Google and Microsoft. This guide will walk you through signing up and signing in to Condense.

## Condense currently uses 2 kinds of authentication methods

1\. **Native Sign-up and Log-in**: Users can create an account using their email and password.

2\. **Single Sign On (SSO)**: Users can sign in using their existing corporate or third-party identity provider credentials (e.g., Google - GSuite, Microsoft-M365).

IMPORTANT

Right now, you will be able to log in or sign up through your work email using Google (GSuite) and Microsoft (M365). We will soon be enabling Single Sign On (SSO) using your work email address for other Identity Providers, too.

### Signing in with Email and Password

**Step 1:** Open your Condense application which will land you on the login page.

![](https://files.gitbook.com/v0/b/spaces%2FvbacpakLuL4x2VBp2174%2Fuploads%2FEdSpy85TmLv5qtENmndO%2Fimage.png)

**Step 2:** Enter your registered email.

**Step 3:** Click on the Next button.

If you have signed up using SSO, you will be redirected to the corresponding SSO account page or else the password field will be displayed

**Step 4:** Enter the password in the password text field

![](https://files.gitbook.com/v0/b/spaces%2FvbacpakLuL4x2VBp2174%2Fuploads%2Fw1ihKCtpOknJPFIsbAiU%2Fimage.png)

**Step 5:** If the credentials are correct, you will be redirected to the dashboard

IMPORTANT!

1. Log-in using email and password will only be available for the user accounts that are created using the regular *sign-up flow* (i.e. email address and password)

2. Users have the option to log in to their respective accounts using the SSO option or the regular *sign-up* *flow* (i.e. email address and password)

3. The '**Change password**' and '**Forgot Password**' will only work for accounts which are signed up using the regular *sign-up* *flow* (i.e. email address and password)
