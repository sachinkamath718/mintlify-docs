**Try Condense for Free** is a self-service evaluation experience that enables you to explore Condense without scheduling a product demonstration or contacting the sales team. After signing up, a pre-provisioned trial environment is automatically assigned, allowing you to immediately begin building and exploring real-time streaming applications.

The trial provides access to the core capabilities of Condense, including stream processing, Kafka management, connectors, transformations, application development, and deployment workflows. The experience is designed to help developers and engineering teams evaluate Condense using realistic workloads with minimal setup.

Each trial environment comes pre-configured with a default workspace, sample Kafka topics, guided onboarding, and demo applications, allowing you to get started quickly and understand the platform through hands-on exploration.

## What's Included

During your trial, you can:

- Build and deploy stream processing applications.
- Explore Kafka Management capabilities.
- Create pipelines using connectors, transformations, and processing utilities.
- Invite team members to collaborate within the same trial environment.
- Access guided onboarding and interactive demo workspaces.
- Evaluate Condense before upgrading to a production deployment.

## Trial Environment

Each successful sign-up is automatically assigned a pre-provisioned trial environment that includes:

- A dedicated Condense trial environment.
- A default **Quickstart** workspace.
- Pre-created Kafka topics for exploration and learning.
- Guided onboarding to help you navigate the platform.
- Appropriate permissions to explore both Condense and Kafka Management capabilities.

## Typical Evaluation Workflow

Most users complete the following journey during their evaluation:

1. Create a Condense account.
2. Verify your email address (for email-based sign-ups).
3. Access the automatically assigned trial environment.
4. Complete the guided onboarding experience.
5. Build and deploy your first streaming application.
6. Invite team members to collaborate, if required.
7. Upgrade to a production deployment when ready.
