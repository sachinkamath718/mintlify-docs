---
title: "Multi-Organization Support"
---

Multi-Organization Support allows a single Condense account to be associated with multiple organizations. This enables users to collaborate across different customers, business units, or projects without maintaining separate accounts.

Each organization remains logically isolated, with its own members, environments, workspaces, and access permissions.

## Benefits

Multi-Organization Support enables you to:

- Access multiple organizations using a single Condense account.
- Collaborate with different teams without creating additional accounts.
- Maintain separate environments and resources for each organization.
- Simplify account management while preserving organization-level isolation.

## How It Works

Your Condense account acts as your identity across the platform.

Once you become a member of an organization, that organization appears alongside any other organizations you are authorized to access. Your permissions are evaluated independently within each organization.

Membership in one organization does not automatically grant access to another organization.

## Organization Isolation

Each organization maintains its own:

- Users and roles
- Environments
- Workspaces
- Applications
- Kafka resources
- Connectors
- Access permissions

Resources are not shared across organizations unless explicitly supported by the platform.

## Organization Roles

Your role is assigned independently within each organization.

For example, you may be:

- An Organization Administrator in one organization.
- An Environment Administrator in another organization.
- A standard user in a third organization.

Your available actions depend on the permissions assigned within the currently active organization.

## Best Practices

- Use a single Condense account across all organizations whenever possible.
- Assign users only the permissions required for their responsibilities.
- Periodically review organization membership and access permissions.
- Remove users who no longer require access to an organization.
